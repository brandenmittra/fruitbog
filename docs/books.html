Thirty-Five Years

The story of Manchester City’s 2010 / 2011 FA Cup winning season.








About the Book

“Thirty Five Years” is the story of Manchester City’s 2010 / 2011 FA Cup winning season. But in reflecting the decades of disappointment and crushed hopes that define the outlook of older Manchester City supporters, the book is also in many ways the story of every season since 1976.

Rather than simply presenting a series of match reports, the lead up to, and the aftermath of, each match are described through the prism of the author’s wildly oscillating emotions – emotions that are permanently linked to a set of collective memories of events that have burned themselves onto Manchester City folklore since 1976 (and before).

Supporters of Manchester City will be able to recognise the sentiments and feelings as the successive highs and lows of the season break over the author like waves crashing onto a beach – this is a roller coaster ride all the way to Wembley!

About the Author

I was born in 1955 in Swinton, Manchester and became a Manchester City supporter after being taken to Maine Road with a friend in 1967. In 2000 I helped to form the Milton Keynes branch of the Manchester City Supporters Club, and continued to help to run the branch until 2011. I live in Towcester, Northamptonshire with my wife and two daughters, one of whom is also a Manchester City supporter (and whose partner is a Manchester United supporter from New York!)
